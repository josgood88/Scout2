Title: AB-3285 (Irwin) Medi-Cal: antipsychotic drugs.

Summary: (Reviewed 3/7/2020)
(Quotations taken directly from the bill's language, or from current code)

This bill would
1. prohibit requiring prior authorization for an antipsychotic drug to treat the serious mental illness of a Medi-Cal enrollee for 365 days after the initial prescription has been dispensed
2. require automatic approval of an antipsychotic drug to treat the serious mental illness of a Medi-Cal enrollee if the department verifies a paid claim that documents a diagnosis of a serious mental illness within 365 days before the date of that prescription
3. exclude an antipsychotic drug to treat serious mental illness from the Medi-Cal programï¿½s limit of 6 drugs per month
4. allow a pharmacist to dispense a 90-day supply or early refill of a prescribed antipsychotic drug if specified criteria are met.

In other words,
1. Starting with initial prescription, no further prior authorization required for a year
2. Once any "serious mental illness" medication payment made for an enrollee, any other antipsychotic drug must be approved
3. No limit on number of drugs enrollee can be taking
4. Large batches may be dispensed

Position: Oppose
This makes it easy for psychiatrists to prescribe large amounts of psychotropics to Medi-Cal enrollees.

ShortSummary: Much easier for psychiatrists to prescribe large amounts of psychotropics to Medi-Cal enrollees.
